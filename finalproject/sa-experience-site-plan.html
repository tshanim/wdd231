Project Site Plan: South Africa Experience

Site Name:

South Africa Experience – A Cultural and Travel Guide

  • This name reflects the purpose of the site: to provide a memorable and comprehensive guide to South Africa’s attractions, culture, and traditions.

Site Purpose:

Sharing South Africa’s Beauty and Diversity

  • The website will highlight major cities such as Johannesburg, Cape Town, and Durban, as well as iconic destinations like Table Mountain, Kruger National Park, and the Drakensberg mountains.
  • It will showcase South African traditions, food, music, and festivals, offering visitors an authentic cultural perspective.
  • Practical information such as weather, currency, safety tips, and the best seasons to visit will help travelers plan their journeys.

Scenarios:

  • What are the top tourist attractions to visit in Johannesburg, Cape Town, and Durban?

    This question will drive content about popular landmarks, cultural spots, and must-see attractions in South Africa’s major cities, including descriptions, images, and recommendations.

  • What are the best outdoor activities and natural wonders to explore in South Africa?

    This scenario will guide content about South Africa’s national parks, beaches, hiking trails, and wildlife experiences, catering to adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts.

Color Scheme:

The color scheme is inspired by South Africa’s landscapes and culture:

  • Caribbean Current (hsla(182, 100%, 23%, 1)): Used for headings and accents, symbolizing the ocean and vitality.
  • Floral White (hsla(47, 43%, 96%, 1)): Used as the background for a clean, pleasant visual experience.
  • Sunset Orange (hsla(15, 85%, 55%, 1)): Used for hover states and highlights, inspired by African sunsets.

Example CSS HSL:

    /* CSS HSL */
    --black: hsla(0, 0%, 0%, 1);
    --caribbean-current: hsla(182, 100%, 23%, 1);
    --sunset-orange: hsla(15, 85%, 55%, 1);
    --floral-white: hsla(47, 43%, 96%, 1);
    --white: hsla(0, 0%, 100%, 1);

Typography:

1. Primary Font: Oswald (sans-serif)

  • Usage: Oswald will be used for all headings to give a bold, modern look and guide users through sections.

2. Secondary Font: Source Sans Pro (sans-serif)

  • Usage: Source Sans Pro will be used for body text, ensuring readability and visual appeal.
  • Applied to: Paragraphs, descriptions of attractions, and interactive elements like links and buttons.
